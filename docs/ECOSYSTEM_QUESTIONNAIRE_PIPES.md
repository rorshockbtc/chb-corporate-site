# CHB Ecosystem Questionnaire - Pipes

## Part 1: Product Identity

### One-liner (10 words or less)
Pipes: Human expertise packaged as reusable, monetizable AI infrastructure.

### Core Problem
The Curation Economy Gap: Domain experts spend years accumulating knowledge but have no scalable way to monetize it. Meanwhile, AI systems are trained on undifferentiated internet data with no accountability, transparency, or compensation to original knowledge creators. Pipes bridges this gap by creating economic infrastructure for human expertise.

Secondary problems solved:
- AI outputs lack transparency about their knowledge sources
- Content creators can't track or earn from how their expertise is used
- Developers need domain-specific AI but lack access to verified expert knowledge
- There's no "namespace system" for human expertise (like domains for websites)

### "Aha Moment"
**For Curators**: When they see their first equity payment arrive—the moment passive income from their expertise becomes real. "My fly fishing knowledge just made me $47 while I slept."

**For Installers/Developers**: When they compare AI output with and without a Pipe installed—seeing a generic response transform into domain-expert-quality content. "This actually sounds like it was written by someone who knows fly fishing."

**For the Marketplace**: When users browse and see the transparent bias statements—realizing they can choose perspectives intentionally rather than getting hidden algorithmic bias.

### Differentiator
- **Namespace ownership**: Like domain names for expertise—`pipe\flyfishing` is yours forever
- **Explicit bias as a feature**: Curators declare their perspective openly rather than pretending objectivity
- **Equity model**: Contributors earn ongoing revenue share, not one-time payments—creating "expertise as equity"
- **Layerable pipes**: Install multiple perspectives simultaneously for nuanced AI output
- **Portable installation**: Works across platforms, AI systems, and applications via simple embed

**Why they use Pipes**: To accelerate their product with domain expertise they don't have internally. Instead of hiring consultants or building training datasets, they install verified expert knowledge into their AI systems instantly.

**Biggest win**: Launching a product that sounds like it was built by industry insiders—without the 6-month learning curve. "Our home brewing app gives advice that sounds like a master brewer because it is."

**Resonant language**:
- "Infrastructure" (they understand building blocks)

- "Equity" (speaks to their investment mindset)
- "Scalable expertise" (growth language)
- "Source of truth" (data integrity matters)
- "Verified" (quality assurance)
- "Zero to one" (Thiel reference—creating new categories)

### Content Creators (Purple/Magenta)
**Why they use Pipes**: To transform their accumulated knowledge—blog posts, tutorials, opinions—into a revenue-generating asset that compounds over time. Instead of trading time for money (sponsorships, courses), they build an expertise asset that earns while they create new content.

**Biggest win**: Seeing revenue arrive from installations they didn't even know about. "Someone in Japan is using my cooking pipe in their recipe app. I just got paid for knowledge I shared three years ago."

**Resonant language**:
- "Your expertise, your rules" (ownership and control)
- "Passive income" (classic creator goal)

### Memory Capturers (Green)
**Why they use Pipes**: To give structure and meaning to the knowledge they naturally accumulate. Memory capturers are collectors—they save articles, bookmark resources, take notes obsessively. Pipes transforms this hoarding instinct into something shareable and valuable.

**Biggest win**: The validation that their "random" knowledge collection has real value. "I've been saving vintage camera articles for 15 years. Now it's a pipe that other people actually pay to use."

**Resonant language**:

## Part 3: Credits & Pricing

### Credit Consumption
*Note: Sparse on exact technical details for patent purposes*

**Concept**: Credits are consumed when a Pipe is "queried"—when an installed application requests knowledge from a curator's expertise. The credit cost varies based on:
- Complexity of the query
- Depth of content retrieved
- Whether AI synthesis is required

**Ballpark**: A simple content retrieval might be 1 credit. An AI-synthesized response drawing from multiple pieces of curated content might be 5-10 credits.

### Usage Patterns
Typical monthly consumption varies by user type:
- **Light installer**: 50-200 credits/month (occasional queries)
- **Active application**: 500-2000 credits/month (integrated into product)
- **High-volume platform**: 5000+ credits/month (powering many users)

**Curator perspective**: Credits consumed = revenue earned. Active pipes with many installations generate steady passive income.

### Value Articulation
"One credit is one moment of expert insight. It's the difference between a generic AI response and one informed by years of someone's real experience. You're not paying for compute—you're paying for curation."

Alternative framing: "A credit is like a micro-royalty to the experts whose knowledge improves your AI."

## Part 4: Ecosystem Integration

### Cross-Product Synergy
**Pipes + Hash**:
Hash captures your personal journey—thoughts, learnings, evolution. Pipes lets you package and share that accumulated wisdom. Hash is the private garden; Pipes is the farmer's market stand. Hash users can discover their natural expertise areas through journaling, then create Pipes from their strongest themes.

**Pipes + Semi**:
Semi provides the AI intelligence layer. When you create a Pipe, Semi helps you discover your biases, suggest relevant content, and optimize for quality. When someone queries a Pipe, Semi synthesizes the curated content into coherent responses. Semi is the brain; Pipes is the curriculum.

**Hash + Semi + Pipes together**:
Your Hash journals reveal your expertise → Semi analyzes your perspective and biases → You create a Pipe packaging that knowledge → Semi powers the queries → You earn equity from your accumulated wisdom.

### Data Sharing
**Hash → Pipes**:
- Personality insights (for curator profiles)
- Topic expertise detection (what you know deeply)
- Writing voice analysis (maintaining authenticity)
- Cognitive patterns (how you think)

**Semi → Pipes**:
- Bias discovery suggestions
- Content recommendations
- Quality scoring
- Query synthesis

**Pipes → Semi**:
- Curated knowledge for training/context
- Expert perspectives for nuanced responses
- Verified sources for citations

### Single Account Benefits
**#1 Reason**: Your entire intellectual identity travels with you. Your Hash reveals who you are, Semi understands how you think, and Pipes lets you share what you know. One account means your AI assistant (Semi) knows your expertise (from Hash) and can help you build your knowledge business (Pipes) with zero friction.

## Part 6: Tone & Messaging

### Brand Voice
Pipes speaks like a trusted insider who's excited to share a secret:

"Your expertise has been working for free. Let's fix that. Every link you've saved, every opinion you've formed, every recommendation you've made—it's all value that's been leaking into the void. Pipes plugs the leak."

"We don't pretend AI is objective. Neither should you. Every Pipe comes with an explicit bias statement because transparency isn't a bug—it's the whole point. Choose your perspectives intentionally."

"Think of a Pipe as a domain name for what you know. `pipe\sourdough` is yours. Own it. Curate it. Profit from it. Your namespace, your rules, your equity."

### Avoid Saying
**Technical jargon that sounds cold**:
- "Machine learning pipeline" (too technical)
- "Data monetization" (sounds extractive)
- "User-generated content" (dismissive of expertise)
- "Crowdsourced" (implies lack of curation)

**Overpromising language**:
- "Passive income" without context (requires real curation)
- "Get rich" or "easy money"
- "Replace your job"
- "AI-powered" as the main value prop (the human expertise IS the value)

**Terms that minimize human contribution**:
- "Content" instead of "expertise" or "knowledge"
- "Upload" instead of "curate"
- "Users" instead of "curators" or "experts"

### :-] Moments
**The namespace claim experience**: Typing `pipe\` and seeing YOUR topic appear in that distinctive monospace pink—it feels like claiming digital territory. The visual satisfaction of ownership.

**The bias transparency wizard**: Instead of hiding bias, we celebrate it. The questionnaire asks "What's your political orientation?" with zero judgment—just genuine curiosity. Curators often laugh at how liberating it is.

**The Perspective Explorer**: Seeing 9 different political perspectives analyze the same event side by side—from Marxist to Libertarian to Centrist—and realizing "oh, Pipes doesn't pick sides. It shows ALL the sides."

**Quality score as a badge of honor**: The star rating and numerical score feel like a game—curators want to improve, not because we're gamifying, but because quality genuinely matters here.

**The equity notification**: When a curator sees "+$12.47 from pipe\vintage-cameras" in their earnings dashboard. It's proof that their weird obsession has value.
